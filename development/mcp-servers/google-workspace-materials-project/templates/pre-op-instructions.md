## The Day Before Surgery

### What to Eat and Drink

- Eat normally throughout the day
- Drink plenty of clear liquids
- {{SPECIAL_DIET_INSTRUCTIONS}}

### Medications

- Take your regular medications unless instructed otherwise
- If you take medications for diabetes, follow these specific instructions: {{DIABETES_INSTRUCTIONS}}

### Preparation

- [ ] Shower or bathe using regular soap
- [ ] {{SPECIAL_SKIN_PREP}} (if ordered by your surgeon)
- [ ] Remove nail polish and artificial nails
- [ ] Do not apply lotion, powder, or deodorant
- [ ] Pack a small bag with: insurance cards, ID, list of medications, glasses (if needed), comfortable loose-fitting clothes

### What NOT to Do

- ❌ No alcohol for 24 hours before surgery
- ❌ No smoking or vaping (nicotine can cause complications)
- ❌ Do not eat or drink anything after midnight (or {{NPO_TIME}}) unless specifically instructed

## Day of Surgery

### Nothing by Mouth (NPO)

**You must have an empty stomach for anesthesia safety.**

- ❌ **NO food** after {{NPO_FOOD_TIME}}
- ❌ **NO liquids** (including water, coffee, gum, mints) after {{NPO_LIQUID_TIME}}
- ✅ Exception: Small sips of water with approved medications (see below)

### Morning Medications

Take these medications with a small sip of water:

{{MORNING_MEDICATIONS}}

Do NOT take:

{{MORNING_MEDICATIONS_TO_SKIP}}

### Personal Care

- [ ] Shower with antibacterial soap if provided
- [ ] Brush teeth (do not swallow water)
- [ ] Do not wear makeup, lotion, perfume, or hair products
- [ ] Remove all jewelry (including piercings)
- [ ] Leave valuables at home

### What to Wear

- Loose, comfortable clothing
- Shirt that buttons in front (for some procedures)
- Comfortable shoes (slip-on preferred)
- Glasses instead of contact lenses

### What to Bring

- [ ] Photo ID
- [ ] Insurance cards
- [ ] List of current medications
- [ ] CPAP machine (if you use one)
- [ ] Glasses or hearing aids (if needed)
- [ ] Any medical equipment you use daily

### What NOT to Bring

- ❌ Jewelry or valuables
- ❌ Large amounts of cash
- ❌ Contact lenses
- ❌ Dentures (unless instructed to bring them)

## If You Get Sick Before Surgery

**Call our office immediately if you develop:**
- Fever over 100.4°F (38°C)
- Cough or cold symptoms
- Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
- New rash or infection
- COVID-19 symptoms or exposure

We may need to reschedule your surgery for your safety.
