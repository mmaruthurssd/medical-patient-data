## Wound Care

### Keeping Your Incision Clean

- Keep the bandage dry and clean for the first 48 hours
- You may shower after 48 hours, but don't soak the incision
- Pat the area dry with a clean towel - don't rub
- Change bandages daily or when they get wet or dirty

### Watch for Signs of Infection

Call us right away if you notice:
- Redness spreading from the incision
- Increased swelling or warmth
- Pus or drainage that smells bad
- Fever over 101°F (38.3°C)
- Opening of the incision

---

## Pain Management

### Your Pain Medication

- Take {{PAIN_MEDICATION}} as prescribed
- Don't wait until pain is severe - take it on schedule for the first few days
- Take with food to prevent stomach upset
- Don't drive or operate machinery while taking narcotic pain medication

### Non-Medication Pain Relief

- Apply ice packs for 20 minutes every 2-3 hours for the first 48 hours
- Keep the surgical area elevated above heart level when possible
- Rest and avoid strenuous activities

## Activity Guidelines

### What You Can Do

- Light walking around the house is encouraged
- Gradually increase activity each day
- Use assistive devices (walker, crutches) as instructed

### What to Avoid

- No lifting anything heavier than {{WEIGHT_LIMIT}} pounds
- No driving while taking narcotic pain medication
- No strenuous exercise or heavy lifting for {{RESTRICTION_WEEKS}} weeks
- Avoid bending, twisting, or reaching overhead

---

## Diet and Nutrition

- Start with clear liquids and progress to regular diet as tolerated
- Drink plenty of water (8-10 glasses per day)
- Eat high-fiber foods to prevent constipation from pain medication
- Avoid alcohol while taking pain medication

## When to Call Your Doctor

### Call During Office Hours For:
- Questions about wound care or activity
- Mild increase in pain not relieved by medication
- Constipation lasting more than 3 days

### Call Immediately or Go to Emergency Room For:
- Severe chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Severe bleeding from incision
- Signs of infection (listed above)
- Severe pain not relieved by medication
- Numbness, tingling, or coldness in surgical area
- Sudden swelling or warmth in your leg (may indicate blood clot)
