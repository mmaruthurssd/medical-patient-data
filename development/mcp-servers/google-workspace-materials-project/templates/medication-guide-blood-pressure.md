## Lifestyle Tips

### To Help Your Medication Work Better:

**Diet:**
- Reduce salt intake (less than 2,300 mg per day)
- Eat more fruits and vegetables
- Choose lean proteins
- Limit alcohol to {{ALCOHOL_LIMIT}}

**Exercise:**
- Aim for 30 minutes of moderate activity most days
- Walking, swimming, or biking are great choices
- Talk to your doctor before starting a new exercise program

**Weight:**
- Maintain a healthy weight
- Even losing 5-10 pounds can help lower blood pressure

**Stress:**
- Practice relaxation techniques
- Get enough sleep (7-8 hours per night)
- Consider meditation or deep breathing exercises

**Smoking:**
- Quit smoking - ask about cessation programs
- Smoking raises blood pressure and reduces medication effectiveness

## Home Blood Pressure Monitoring

### How to Check Your Blood Pressure at Home:

1. Sit quietly for 5 minutes before measuring
2. Sit with back supported and feet flat on floor
3. Rest arm at heart level on a table
4. Place cuff on bare arm
5. Take 2-3 readings, 1 minute apart
6. Record all readings with date and time

### Your Blood Pressure Goals:
- **Target:** Less than {{BP_TARGET}} (e.g., "130/80 mmHg")
- **Current:** {{CURRENT_BP}}

### When to Call Your Doctor:
- Blood pressure above {{BP_HIGH_THRESHOLD}} on multiple readings
- Blood pressure below {{BP_LOW_THRESHOLD}}
- Symptoms of high blood pressure: severe headache, chest pain, vision changes
- Symptoms of low blood pressure: dizziness, fainting, confusion
